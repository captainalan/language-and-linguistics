Latin, Level 6

Here we introduce personal pronouns.

First Person

For the dative, both mihi and mī are valid forms.

Case Singular Plural
Nominative ego nōs
Genitive meī nostrum, nostrī
Dative mihi; mī nōbīs
Accusative mē nōs
Ablative mē nōbīs

Second Person

Case Singular Plural
Nominative tū vōs
Genitive tuī vestrum, vestrī
Dative tibi vōbīs
Accusative tē vōs
Ablative tē vōbīs

Third Person

Note there are different third person pronouns for "he", "she", and "it".

is "he"

Case Singular Plural
Nominative is eī; eī
Genitive ēius eōrum
Dative ī eīs
Accusative eum eōs
Ablative eī eīs

ea "she"

Case Singular Plural
Nominative ea eae
Genitive ēius eārum
Dative eī eīs
Accusative eam eās
Ablative eā eīs

id "it"

Case Singular Plural
Nominative id ea
Genitive ēius eōrum
Dative eī eīs
Accusative id ea
Ablative eō eīs
